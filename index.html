Explanation

I used a few different tricks to make things display nicely and render faster. There's also a lot of other HTML and CSS, but the above code snippet shows the core of the algorithm.

It starts off by defining a function, that returns an array containing the coordinates of a random dot.

I then define isInCircle, to take in the coordinates of that dot, and check if the dot is in the circle, I then calculate the distance in the x and y axis from the dot to the origin, and then using the distance formula get the total distance. If the distance is less than or equal to 500 it means it’s inside the circle, (the circle has radius 500).

It then counts how many dots are within the circle for every iteration, and then divides this by the total amount of dotes and multiplies it by 4.

Other Thoughts

One thing that I noticed was the the improvement in the accuracy of the approximation was very small from 1-100 million in amount of dots used.

It seems as tough the improvement might be logarithmic, as if there is a 10x improvement in accuracy between 100-100 000 and the same 10x improvement between 100 000 and 100 million. The computer power needed to compute so high values seems very ineffective for approximating π to more than 3-5 digits.
